\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Background}

The financial technology (FinTech) sector has experienced rapid and transformative growth over the past decade. Digital payment platforms, neo-banks, peer-to-peer lending portals, robo-advisors, and insurance-technology companies now collectively serve billions of customers worldwide. This digital shift has dramatically lowered the barriers to switching between providers, fundamentally altering competitive dynamics. Unlike traditional branch-based banking, where friction and inertia often kept customers loyal, FinTech platforms compete on user experience, fee structure, and product breadth---factors that customers can evaluate and act upon within minutes using a smartphone.

In this hypercompetitive environment, \textit{customer churn}---the decision by a customer to discontinue using a platform's services---emerges as one of the most strategically significant phenomena that operators must manage. Industry research consistently indicates that acquiring a new customer costs five to seven times more than retaining an existing one \cite{reichheld1996loyalty}. Furthermore, even a modest improvement in retention rate of five percentage points can increase profitability by 25--95\% depending on the business model \cite{reichheld2000ecommerce}. Despite its importance, accurately predicting \textit{which} customers are at risk of churning remains a challenging analytical problem.

Traditional churn management relied on rule-based heuristics: customers who had not logged in for 90 days, or whose monthly transaction volume had declined below a threshold, were flagged by customer service teams. These approaches suffer from poor precision---flagging too many customers who would not have churned anyway---and poor recall---missing customers who churn without obvious behavioural signals. Machine learning (ML) methods, which can jointly exploit dozens of features and detect non-linear interaction effects, offer substantially better predictive power for this task \cite{burez2009handling}.

\section{Problem Statement}

Despite the availability of rich transactional and behavioural data, many FinTech operators lack a systematic, data-driven churn prediction capability. Key challenges include:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Class imbalance}: In most FinTech portfolios, only 15--25\% of customers churn in any given period. Standard classifiers optimised for accuracy tend to predict the majority class (non-churn) for most observations, yielding high nominal accuracy but poor actionable recall on the minority (churn) class \cite{chawla2002smote}.

    \item \textbf{Feature engineering complexity}: Raw data stored in CRM and transactional databases must be transformed into informative features. This requires domain knowledge about which behavioural patterns are predictive of future churn \cite{coussement2008churn}.

    \item \textbf{Model interpretability}: Financial institutions are subject to regulatory requirements (e.g., GDPR, model risk management guidelines) that demand explainable AI. Black-box models cannot be deployed without adequate interpretation mechanisms \cite{lundberg2017shap}.

    \item \textbf{Operational integration}: Even a highly accurate offline model has no business value unless it can be integrated into customer engagement workflows---such as CRM systems, marketing automation platforms, or customer service dashboards---in near-real-time.

    \item \textbf{Concept drift}: Customer behaviour evolves over time; models trained on historical data degrade in performance without periodic retraining and monitoring \cite{gama2014conceptdrift}.
\end{enumerate}

This project addresses all five challenges by developing an end-to-end churn prediction system that is accurate, interpretable, and designed for production deployment.

\section{Objectives of the Project Work}

The primary objective of this project is to design, develop, and evaluate a machine learning-based customer churn prediction system tailored to the FinTech domain. The specific objectives are as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item To conduct a thorough exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the bank customer churn dataset, identifying key statistical properties, data quality issues, and preliminary predictors of churn.

    \item To engineer a comprehensive set of discriminative features from raw demographic, account, and behavioural data, including interaction terms and aggregate statistics.

    \item To implement and compare six state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms---Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), Support Vector Machine, and Artificial Neural Network---under consistent experimental conditions.

    \item To investigate and mitigate the adverse effects of class imbalance using SMOTE, Random Over-Sampling, and class-weight adjustment strategies, evaluating their impact on model performance.

    \item To perform rigorous hyperparameter optimisation using Bayesian search and stratified cross-validation, and to assess model performance using a multi-metric suite including Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, AUC-ROC, and AUC-PR.

    \item To apply SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) for model-agnostic feature importance analysis and individual prediction explanation, satisfying interpretability requirements.

    \item To architect and implement the best-performing model as a RESTful microservice, containerised with Docker and designed for integration into existing FinTech CRM platforms.

    \item To quantify the potential business impact of the proposed system in terms of estimated revenue retention and return on investment for proactive churn intervention campaigns.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scope of the Project}

This project operates within the following scope boundaries:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dataset}: The study uses the publicly available Bank Customer Churn dataset (10{,}000 records, 14 features) as a representative proxy for a FinTech customer portfolio.

    \item \textbf{Algorithms}: Six supervised binary classification algorithms are implemented using the Python scikit-learn library and the XGBoost framework. Deep learning architectures beyond shallow ANNs are outside scope.

    \item \textbf{Deployment}: A Flask-based REST API is developed and containerised using Docker. Cloud-native deployment (e.g., Kubernetes orchestration) is discussed as future work.

    \item \textbf{Data privacy}: No real customer data is used. All experiments are conducted on publicly available data, and the system design conforms to GDPR data minimisation principles.

    \item \textbf{Temporal modelling}: This project does not address time-series survival analysis or recurrent neural network-based temporal churn modelling, which are identified as future research directions.
\end{itemize}
