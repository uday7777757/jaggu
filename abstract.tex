\chapter*{Abstract}

Customer churn---the phenomenon of customers discontinuing their engagement with a financial service provider---represents one of the most critical business challenges facing modern FinTech platforms. Acquiring a new customer typically costs five to seven times more than retaining an existing one, making early and accurate churn prediction a high-value capability. This project presents a comprehensive machine learning-based framework for predicting customer churn in a FinTech platform environment, covering the entire data science pipeline from raw data ingestion to production-ready deployment.

The study employs a publicly available bank customer churn dataset comprising 10{,}000 records with fourteen features, including demographic information, account tenure, balance, product usage, and activity indicators. Extensive exploratory data analysis revealed a significant class imbalance, with only approximately 20\% of customers labelled as churned. To address this imbalance, multiple resampling strategies were evaluated, including Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE), Random Over-Sampling (ROS), and class-weight adjustments. The preprocessing pipeline encompassed missing-value imputation, categorical encoding (one-hot and label encoding), and standard scaling of continuous features.

Six machine learning algorithms were trained and compared: Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), Support Vector Machine (SVM), and a shallow Artificial Neural Network (ANN). Hyperparameter optimisation was performed using Bayesian search and five-fold stratified cross-validation. Model performance was evaluated using a multi-metric suite---accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC-ROC), and Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve (AUC-PR)---to provide a holistic view of predictive quality under class imbalance.

The XGBoost classifier with SMOTE resampling achieved the best overall performance: accuracy of 87.4\%, recall of 81.2\%, F1-score of 79.8\%, and AUC-ROC of 0.923. Feature importance analysis identified account balance, age, number of products, and estimated salary as the four most predictive attributes. SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values were computed to provide model-agnostic, interpretable explanations of individual predictions---a requirement for regulatory compliance in financial services.

The resulting system is packaged as a RESTful microservice using Flask, containerised with Docker, and designed for integration into existing FinTech CRM and customer-engagement platforms. A business impact analysis demonstrates that proactive retention campaigns targeted using the model predictions could reduce annual churn-related revenue loss by an estimated 18--24\%.

This report documents all aspects of the system: motivation and literature context, system analysis and design, implementation details, testing methodology, experimental results, and directions for future enhancement. The work demonstrates that well-engineered machine learning pipelines, combined with principled handling of class imbalance and model interpretability, can deliver actionable and trustworthy churn predictions for real-world financial applications.
