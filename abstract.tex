\chapter*{Abstract}

Customer churn---the phenomenon whereby clients discontinue their relationship with a service
provider---represents one of the most pressing challenges confronting modern financial technology
(FinTech) organisations. In subscription-based and transactional business models, the cost of
acquiring a new customer typically exceeds the cost of retaining an existing one by a factor of
five to twenty-five, making proactive churn management a strategic priority. Despite the critical
importance of churn prevention, many FinTech platforms continue to rely on rule-based systems or
simple logistic regression models that fail to capture the complex, non-linear behavioural patterns
indicative of imminent customer departure.

This report presents the design, implementation, and evaluation of a comprehensive machine-learning
pipeline for customer churn prediction tailored to a FinTech platform context. The proposed system
ingests a heterogeneous dataset comprising transactional records, product-usage logs, customer
demographics, and customer-support interaction histories. Rigorous data preprocessing steps---including
missing-value imputation, outlier treatment, normalisation, and encoding of categorical
variables---are applied to produce a clean analytical dataset. An extensive feature engineering
phase generates forty-seven derived attributes that capture temporal usage trends, recency--frequency--monetary
(RFM) behavioural profiles, and rolling-window statistics over thirty-, sixty-, and ninety-day intervals.

To address the inherent class imbalance present in real-world churn datasets (where churners
typically constitute fewer than twenty percent of the customer base), the Synthetic Minority
Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) is applied in conjunction with Tomek link under-sampling. Seven
machine-learning classifiers are evaluated: Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest,
Gradient Boosting, Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), LightGBM, and a Multi-Layer Perceptron
(MLP) neural network. Hyperparameter optimisation is performed via five-fold stratified
cross-validated Bayesian search, and the final model is selected based on the area under the
Receiver Operating Characteristic curve (ROC-AUC), F1-score, and business-oriented precision--recall
trade-offs.

The XGBoost classifier with SMOTE preprocessing achieves the best performance, recording a
ROC-AUC of 0.9247, an F1-score of 0.8431, a recall of 0.8812, and a precision of 0.8086 on the
held-out test set. SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) are employed to provide model
explainability, identifying the most influential churn predictors: days since last transaction,
monthly login frequency, outstanding balance to credit-limit ratio, number of support tickets
raised in the past ninety days, and product-diversification index.

A business-impact analysis demonstrates that targeted intervention for the top-decile at-risk
customers---representing 68\% of actual churners---can yield an estimated annual revenue retention
of \$4.2~million and a return on investment exceeding 340\% relative to campaign costs. The system
is containerised using Docker and exposed via a REST API, enabling seamless integration with
existing customer relationship management (CRM) platforms.

In conclusion, the proposed end-to-end pipeline successfully operationalises advanced machine
learning for churn prevention in the FinTech domain. Future enhancements include the incorporation
of graph-neural-network-based social influence modelling, real-time streaming inference, and
federated learning to preserve data privacy across partner institutions.
